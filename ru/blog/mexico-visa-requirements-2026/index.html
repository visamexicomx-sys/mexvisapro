Визовые Требования Мексики 2026: Кому Нужна Виза и Как Подать

Полный разбор: какие национальности нуждаются в визе для Мексики, правила безвизового въезда, туристическая карта FMM и варианты визы резидентства.

June 4, 2026 · 8 min read · MexVisa Pro Team

Planning a trip to Mexico — or thinking about moving here? The visa rules depend on your nationality, how long you plan to stay, and what you plan to do. Here's the complete guide for 2026, including the latest updates from Mexico's INM.

Visa-Free Countries (No Visa Required)

Citizens of 69 countries can enter Mexico without a visa for tourism, business visits, or medical treatment for up to 180 days. These include:

  • North America: United States, Canada
  • European Union: All 27 member states
  • United Kingdom, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland
  • Asia-Pacific: Japan, South Korea, Australia, New Zealand, Singapore
  • South America: Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Peru, Uruguay
  • Others: Israel, UAE, Turkey, and more

You only need a valid passport and the FMM tourist card.

The Visa Waiver: Enter with a US, Canada, UK, Japan, or Schengen Visa

Even if your country normally requires a visa for Mexico, you may enter visa-free if you hold a valid, unexpired visa or permanent residence card from:

  • United States
  • Canada
  • United Kingdom
  • Japan
  • Any Schengen country
  • Pacific Alliance countries (Chile, Colombia, Peru)

Example: A Chinese citizen with a valid US B1/B2 visa can enter Mexico for tourism without applying for a separate Mexican visa. An Indian citizen with a valid Schengen visa qualifies too.

Countries That Need a Visa

If your country is not on the visa-free list and you don't hold a qualifying visa from the countries above, you must apply for a Mexican visa before travel. Major countries that require a visa include:

  • India (unless holding a valid US, Schengen, UK, Canada, or Japan visa)
  • China (unless holding a qualifying visa)
  • Philippines, Vietnam, Indonesia
  • Most African nations
  • Cuba, Haiti, Dominican Republic
  • Several Middle Eastern countries

The FMM Tourist Card

All visitors to Mexico — even visa-free nationals — need an FMM (Forma Migratoria Multiple). This is Mexico's immigration form that records your entry and authorized stay.

  • Fee: 983 MXN (~$57 USD) as of 2026
  • Air travelers: The fee is often included in your airfare, and some airlines process it electronically
  • Land border: You must fill out the physical form and pay at the border if traveling beyond the border zone (20-30 km)
  • Maximum stay: Up to 180 days, but the immigration officer decides your actual duration

Important: Always check the number of days written on your FMM. Officers sometimes give 30 or 90 days instead of the full 180. If you need more time, politely explain your plans or apply for a residency visa.

Types of Mexican Visas

Tourist Visa (Visitante Sin Permiso para Actividades Remuneradas)

For tourism, business meetings, medical treatment, or attending events. Valid for up to 180 days. No work allowed. Applied for at a Mexican consulate in your home country.

Temporary Resident Visa (Residente Temporal)

For those planning to live in Mexico 1-4 years. Allows you to open bank accounts, get an RFC, and apply for a work permit. 2026 income requirement: ~$4,400 USD/month or ~$72,000 in savings.

Permanent Resident Visa (Residente Permanente)

Indefinite stay in Mexico. Available through high income/savings, family ties, or after 4 years of temporary residency. 2026 income requirement: ~$7,400 USD/month or ~$300,000 in savings.

Work Visa

Required if you'll be employed by a Mexican company. Your employer must initiate the process through INM. Can be combined with temporary or permanent residency.

How to Apply for a Mexican Visa

  1. Schedule an appointment at the Mexican consulate nearest to your home address
  2. Gather documents: passport, application form, photo, financial proof (for residency), employment letter (for work visa)
  3. Attend interview and submit documents
  4. Wait 10-15 business days for processing
  5. Pick up your passport with visa sticker
  6. Enter Mexico within 180 days and register at INM within 30 days (for residency visas)

Passport Requirements

All visitors must present a valid, unexpired passport or travel document. While Mexico does not officially require 6 months of remaining validity, some airlines enforce this rule. We recommend having at least 6 months of validity to avoid issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do US citizens need a visa for Mexico?

No. US citizens enter visa-free for up to 180 days. You only need a valid passport and the FMM form. For longer stays, you need a residency visa.

What is the FMM and do I still need one?

Yes. The FMM is Mexico's immigration form required for all visitors. The fee is ~983 MXN ($57 USD) in 2026. Air travelers often have it processed electronically.

Can I enter Mexico with a US visa from another country?

Yes. Mexico waives the tourist visa for holders of a valid US, Canadian, UK, Japanese, or Schengen visa — regardless of nationality.

How long can I stay in Mexico without a visa?

Up to 180 days for visa-exempt nationals, but the immigration officer decides your actual stay duration at entry. Always check your FMM.
