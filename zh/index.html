Mexico Immigration Law: Key Milestones

Understanding the legal framework that shapes your path to residency

1974
General Population Law

Mexico established its foundational immigration framework under the Ley General de Poblacion, regulating entry, stay, and departure of foreigners.

2004
FMM Replaces FM-T

The Multiple Immigration Form (FMM) replaced the old FM-T tourist card, streamlining entry for millions of visitors.

2011
New Migration Law

The landmark Ley de Migracion was enacted, creating the modern visa categories: Temporary Resident, Permanent Resident, and Visitor.

2012
INM Restructured

The National Immigration Institute (INM) was reorganized under the Interior Ministry with modernized digital systems for visa processing.

2014
SIRE Digital Platform

INM launched the electronic immigration system (SIRE), allowing online applications and digital tracking of residency procedures.

2021
Remote Work Visa Era

Post-pandemic Mexico became a global hub for digital nomads. Temporary residency applications from remote workers surged 300%.

2024
Updated Income Requirements

Mexico raised the financial solvency thresholds for temporary and permanent residency, reflecting economic changes since the original 2012 law.

2026
Digital INM Modernization

INM continues expanding digital services with online appointments, electronic visa tracking, and streamlined renewal procedures across Quintana Roo.
